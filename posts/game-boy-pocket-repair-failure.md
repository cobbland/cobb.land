I wasn't even shopping for a Game Boy Pocket. I was looking for a good deal on a Game Boy Camera. The best deal I saw at the time was an auction for a bundle that included the Camera and a pair of Pockets (plus a Game Link Cable, GameShark, and Pelican Flip-n-Light). It started with a modest $35 bid, but by evening I was $92 in and the proud owner of way more Game Boy stuff than I had planned on getting. But it was all okay because I could fix them up, sell one to recoup my costs, and keep one to use with the Camera.

According to the listing, the Game Boys needed new screens—the original ones being "sun burned"—but worked otherwise. So I did some research and learned that the fix was just a "simple" matter of removing the top layer of polarizing film, scraping off some adhesive, and replacing the film with a sheet of after-market polarizer. The only downside to the opporation being the newly refurbished screens would have a greenish hue more like the original Game Boy. Which was fine with me, because my planned on getting a DMG-style shell for the one I was planning on keeping anyway.

Should be easy!

Well, when the package arrived, neither of the Game Boy Pockets would turn on. With a little fiddling, they eventually both did, but only one actually showed the logo screen and chimed.

I had my work cut out for me, but I was undeterred.

<img class="gb" src="/images/IMG_PC02.png">

And here is where I made my first mistake. Instead of taking one apart and working on it until I either fixed it or broke it beyond repair, I opened them both.

<img class="gb" src="/images/IMG_PC03.png">

(As an aside, it turns out it isn't the polarizing film that get's burned—it's the adhesive: LOCA, or liquid optically-clear adhesive. In this case, I don't think it's *quite* optically-clear, because it seems to be the adhesive that makes the Game Boy Pocket's screen gray instead of green.)

Now, I'm in for a few hundred dollars worth of (hopefully mostly functional) Game Boys and replacement parts. Fingers crossed.

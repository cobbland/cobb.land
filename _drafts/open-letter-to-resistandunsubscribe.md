# An Open Letter to Resist and Unsubscribe dot com

- https://www.resistandunsubscribe.com
- To hello@profgalloway.com

I'm writing concerning resistandunsubscribe.com. There, an asterisk sits beside the Meta subheading, seemingly innocent but perhaps the most significant mark on the whole page. That purposeful punctuation points to a footnote: 

> \[W\]e'd boycott Instagram too if we could, but we need it to get this message to you. ...

On that page, there is talk about power. It says that American's have a powerful weapon at their disposal. It calls for an economic strike as a way to wield that weapon. But even in so doing, it shows where the power really resides. Even during an attempted strike, even when said strike is directed—among others—at Meta, it just can't be done without their special social media platform. The page says it would be impossible without Instagram. Buddy, that's power right there.

I cheer for the success of this economic strike. I myself am a participant. And like the page implies, we are indeed at a crossroads. But it's not a four-way intersection, with citizens on one road and the economy on the other. No, another road meets here. And at the risk of mixing the metaphor, there is another path one could take.

Assuming my math is right, that page has been visited about 1,166,666 last time I checked (running your impact calculation in reverse). That's 1,166,666 times the opportunity to lessen Meta's stranglehold on the social web came and went. Instead, with the admission of helplessness without Instagram, that grip has only tightened.
